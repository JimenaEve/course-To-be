4. Grammar Rules

4.1 Affirmative

4.1 Affirmative

It is used to affirm something or express facts, routines, and general truths.

Structure:

●        Subject + verb (base form) + complement
Examples:

 I play soccer                        She reads books                    They go to school

Note: For he/she/it, -s or -es is added to the verb

 He plays the guitar              She watches TV

4.2 Negative

4.2 Negative

It is used to deny something.

Structure:

●        Subject + do/does + not + verb (base form) + complement
Examples:

I do not (don't) like coffee.             She does not (doesn't) play tennis.        They do not go to the gym.

Note:

●        Usamos do not (don't) con I/you/we/they

●        Usamos does not (doesn't) con he/she/it

4.3 Interrogative

4.3 Interrogative

It is used to ask questions.

 

Structure:

●        Do/Does + subject + verb (base form) + complement?
Examples:

Do you like pizza?                        

Does he work here?

Note:

●        We use Do con I/you/we/they

●        We use Does with he/she/it
